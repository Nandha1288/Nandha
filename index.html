01 / About

Who I Am

A driven Ph.D. researcher at the intersection of computational biology and experimental microbiology, specializing in the fight against antimicrobial resistance.

My work focuses on Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) — one of the world's most dangerous superbugs. I leverage subtractive genomics, machine learning, molecular docking, and multi-epitope vaccine design to identify novel drug targets and therapeutic candidates.

Beyond computation, I bridge the gap between in silico predictions and real-world laboratory validation through antimicrobial assays — ensuring that discoveries translate from code to cure.

I am actively seeking an Assistant Professor position in Biotechnology where I can contribute to both cutting-edge research and the next generation of scientists.
